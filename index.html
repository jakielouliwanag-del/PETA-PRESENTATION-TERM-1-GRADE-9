Performance Task #1: The Campus Redesign

Welcome, junior campus planners! Help improve and beautify a designated area using geometric concepts. Create a practical, safe, and visually appealing proposal.

  • Group: 3-4 members
  • Points: 36

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Site Inspection

  • Visit your assigned or chosen specific area on campus.
  • Observe existing pathways, open spaces, gardens, benches, trees, and buildings.
  • Take a picture of the current layout.
  • Identify areas that can be improved through geometric redesign.

2. Planning & Design

Plan as a group. Consider:

  • Function: Quiet study corner, outdoor learning, landscaped garden, safer pathway, waiting area.
  • Aesthetics: Geometric arrangements (Parallel walkways, Perpendicular pathways, Symmetrical layouts, Decorative patterns).
  • Elements: Walkways, gardens, benches, planters, signage.

Design Requirements

Your proposed campus redesign must demonstrate geometric concepts learned in Unit 1.

Mandatory Inclusions:
  1. At least one pair of parallel lines.
  2. At least one pair of perpendicular lines.
  3. At least one transversal intersecting a pair of parallel lines.
  4. Label at least five points using proper notation.
  5. Identify and label at least two angle relationships.
  6. Clearly indicate these components on the visual layout.

3. Creating the Visual Layout

On Letter-sized or A4 bond paper, create a neat, colorful, and detailed drawing of your proposal.

The drawing must:

  • Include all proposed features (pathways, gardens, etc.).
  • Clearly label each section and feature using geometric notation.
  • Show required concepts: parallel lines, perpendicular lines, transversal, and angle relationships.
  • Use color, shading, symbols, or patterns to distinguish areas.
  • Be neat, organized, visually appealing, and easy to understand.

4. Geometric Construction Analysis

On a separate sheet of paper, perform mathematical tasks with justifications.

Construct & Explain

Demonstrate the construction of:

  • One pair of parallel lines
  • One pair of perpendicular lines

Explain how these constructions were used in your campus redesign.

Angle Relationships

Identify and classify the angle pairs found in your design. Examples:

  • Complementary
  • Supplementary
  • Vertical
  • Adjacent
  • Linear Pair
  • Corresponding
  • Alt. Interior
  • Alt. Exterior
  • Same-Side Interior

5. Reasoning and Proof

Justify why the area needs redesign and explain improvements.

Sample Reasoning Structure:

Claim: Existing layout causes unnecessary crossing during dismissal.

Evidence: Observation showed students crossing paths, creating congestion.

Justification: Redesign uses parallel walkways and a perpendicular connector to improve movement.

Proof/Support: The proposed pathway intersects at a right angle. Therefore, m∠1 = m∠2 = m∠3 = m∠4 = 90°. EF ⊥ GH.

Documentation: Photo showing open area crossing.

Final Report Assembly: Title Page, Visual Layout, Construction Sheet, Reasoning & Proof Sheet.
